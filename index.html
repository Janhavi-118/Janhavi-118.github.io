Research

My research explores how mechanisms inspired by human brain learning can inform adaptive robotic systems. I am interested in reinforcement learning, neuromorphic perception, and multi-agent robotics, with a focus on understanding how structured learning dynamics enable stability and continual adaptation in embodied systems.

Through projects spanning neuromodulated reinforcement learning, geometric perception, and decentralized swarm control, I study how learning mechanisms can enable robots to coordinate, adapt to uncertainty, and retain useful behavior while acquiring new skills.
