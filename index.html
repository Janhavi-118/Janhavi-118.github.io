Research

My research explores how mechanisms inspired by human brain learning can inform adaptive robotic systems. I am particularly interested in reinforcement learning, neuromorphic perception, and multi-agent robotics, with a focus on how structured learning dynamics enable stability and continual adaptation in embodied systems.

Across my work in neuromodulated reinforcement learning, geometric perception, and decentralized swarm control, I study how learning mechanisms allow robots to coordinate under uncertainty while retaining useful behaviors over long horizons.
